# Weekly Learning Rhythm

> *The weekly structure that keeps the daily loop sustainable.*

---

## The Week

```mermaid
gantt
    title Sustainable Week
    dateFormat X
    axisFormat %a
    section Mon-Fri
    Daily Loop x2-3 :a1, 0, 5d
    section Sat
    Loop x1 + weekly review :a2, 5d, 1d
    section Sun
    Rest :a3, 6d, 1d
```

- **Mon-Fri**: 2-3 daily loops per day = 4-7.5 hours of deep work
- **Sat**: 1-2 loops + weekly review (1 hour)
- **Sun**: rest; light reading OK; no deep work

---

## The Weekly Review (Saturday, 60 min)

The weekly review is the *meta*-practice. Without it, the daily loops drift.

### Step 1 — Aggregate the week (10 min)

Open your [[Daily-Learning-Log]] entries from the week. For each:

- Total hours of deep work?
- Concepts learned?
- Schemas acquired/extended?
- Resources triaged? Read?
- Builds progressed?

Compute totals:
- Deep work hours: __
- Papers read: __
- Builds progressed: __
- SR cards added: __
- Saturation events: __

### Step 2 — Reflect on the week (15 min)

Answer these questions in your log:

1. What did I learn this week that I didn't know before?
2. What did I try that didn't work?
3. What's getting stuck? Why?
4. What's flowing well? Why?
5. Am I on track with my [[The-3-7-Year-Arc|roadmap]]?

### Step 3 — Adjust the schedule (10 min)

Based on the reflection:

- Should I increase or decrease deep work hours?
- Should I switch domains?
- Should I change the daily mix (more build, less read)?
- Should I change my [[Environmental-Design|environment]]?

Make a *specific* adjustment for next week. Not "do better" — "no Twitter before noon" or "add 30 min of algorithms problems to Tues/Thu."

### Step 4 — Plan next week (15 min)

Sketch next week's blocks:

```markdown
## Week of YYYY-MM-DD

Mon: Block A (paper), Block B (build)
Tue: Block A (build), Block B (algorithms)
Wed: Block A (paper), Block B (paper)
Thu: Block A (build), Block B (algorithms)
Fri: Block A (review + SR), Block B (build)
Sat: 1 block + weekly review
Sun: rest
```

Don't over-plan. Leave room for adaptation.

### Step 5 — Update reading list (10 min)

- Move resources between Tier 1 / Tier 2 / Tier 3 based on what you learned
- Add new resources you encountered
- Remove resources you've decided to skip

## The Monthly Consolidation

Once per month (last Saturday of the month), extend the weekly review:

### Step 1 — Re-read your month's notes (30 min)

Skim all [[Concept-Note-Template|concept notes]] from the month. Look for:
- Connections you didn't notice at the time
- Gaps in your schemas
- Topics that need more depth

### Step 2 — Build a concept map (30 min)

Use [[Concept-Mapping-Protocol]] to draw the relationships between everything you learned this month.

### Step 3 — Identify the meta-patterns (15 min)

What kept recurring? What did you struggle with most? What insights would you share with your past self from 4 weeks ago?

### Step 4 — Adjust the roadmap (15 min)

Are you on track with [[The-3-7-Year-Arc|your multi-year plan]]? Should you accelerate, slow down, or pivot?

## The Quarterly Refactor

Every 3 months, do a deeper refactor (see [[Review-and-Refactor-Cycle]]):

- Re-read the [[MOC-Foundations]] notes
- Re-evaluate your reading list
- Take a week off (see [[Burnout-Prevention]])
- Decide if your [[The-3-7-Year-Arc|roadmap]] needs revision

---

## Common Anti-Patterns

- ❌ Skipping the weekly review ("I'll do it next week") — drift compounds
- ❌ Treating the weekly review as a status report — it's for adjustment, not reporting
- ❌ Not making specific adjustments — "do better" isn't an adjustment
- ❌ Over-planning — leave 30% slack for surprises
- ❌ Skipping rest day — burnout risk
