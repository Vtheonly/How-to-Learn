# ⚖️ MOC — Cognitive Load Management

> *How to sustain high-volume technical consumption without burning out.*

---

## The Core Problem

> *Consuming highly abstract, complex systems information is mentally exhausting and highly prone to causing cognitive fatigue.*

The same mechanisms that enable high-velocity learning ([[Long-Term-Working-Memory]], [[Deliberate-Practice]]) also impose a metabolic cost. Brain energy consumption rises sharply during deliberate practice. Sustained without recovery, this produces:

- Working memory saturation (acute)
- Cognitive fatigue (sub-acute, days)
- Burnout (chronic, weeks-months)

The skill is not "pushing through." The skill is *designing* your learning so saturation is rare, fatigue is bounded, and burnout is impossible.

## The Three Timescales of Load Management

| Timescale | Phenomenon | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Minutes | WM saturation | Stop, switch task, recover |
| Hours | Cognitive fatigue | End session; sleep |
| Days-weeks | Burnout | Reduce load; rest; sleep; exercise |

Most learners confuse these. They think "pushing through" WM saturation is discipline; it's actually destructive. They think burnout is "weakness"; it's a physiological signal.

---

## The Single Most Important Number

> **Sustainable deliberate practice ceiling: ~4-5 hours/day, 5-6 days/week.**

Beyond this, quality collapses and burnout risk rises sharply. Most elite practitioners do less than this — 2-4 hours of *truly* focused work per day.

The "10 hour days" you hear about in startup mythology are almost always a mix of:
- Deliberate practice (1-3 hours)
- Performance / work (4-6 hours)
- Email, meetings, Slack (2-3 hours)

Don't try to do 10 hours of deliberate practice. You will fail and burn out.
