# 🧱 MOC — Foundations

> *The cognitive-science bedrock under high-velocity technical learning.*

This section answers: **what does the research actually say about how experts learn?** Everything else in the vault is built on these frameworks.

---

## The Six Anchor Theories

| Theory | Author | Core claim | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cognitive Load Theory | Sweller (1988) | Working memory is the bottleneck; design instruction to minimize extraneous load | [[Cognitive-Load-Theory]] |
| Schema Theory | Rumelhart (1980); Anderson (1977) | Knowledge is stored as structured patterns; experts have richer schemas | [[Schema-Theory-and-Anderson]] |
| Long-Term Working Memory | Ericsson & Kintsch (1995) | Experts bypass WM limits via LTM retrieval cues | [[Long-Term-Working-Memory]] |
| Structure Mapping Theory | Gentner (1983) | Analogy = structural alignment between two domains | [[Structure-Mapping-Theory]] |
| Cognitive Flexibility Theory | Spiro (1988) | Complex ill-structured domains require criss-crossed cases | [[Cognitive-Flexibility-Theory]] |
| Expert Performance Research | Ericsson, Krampe, Tesch-Römer (1993) | Deliberate practice, not innate talent, produces expertise | [[Expertise-Research-Ericsson]] |

---

## The Three Mechanisms That Make High Velocity Possible

1. **Chunking into LTM** → see [[Long-Term-Working-Memory]]. Experts store ~50,000 domain chunks in LTM, bypassing the 4±1 WM limit. Reading becomes recognition, not decoding.

2. **Schema-driven perception** → see [[Schema-Theory-and-Anderson]]. Experts "see" structure (state machines, ownership transfer, monotonic clocks) where novices see surface syntax. This is why a senior engineer reads a 100k LOC codebase in a day.

3. **Engineered practice** → see [[Deliberate-Practice]]. Talent is a multiplier of ~1.3x. Practice quality is a multiplier of ~10x. Expertise emerges from thousands of hours spent *at the edge of competence* with feedback.
