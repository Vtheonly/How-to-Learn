# 🗺️ MOC — Schema Construction

> *How to build robust, transferable mental models.*

---

## The Core Problem

> *Isolated facts are quickly forgotten.*

A learner who reads 100 papers without schema construction remembers ~5 of them. A learner who reads 100 papers *with* schema construction remembers ~70, and can apply their schemas to papers 101-200 without re-reading.

Schema construction is the *multiplier* of learning. Everything else is additive.

## The Three Schema Operations

All schema construction is one of three operations:

1. **Acquisition**: build a new schema from scratch (e.g., first encounter with "monad")
2. **Elaboration**: add detail to an existing schema (e.g., learning that monads satisfy three laws)
3. **Integration**: connect two existing schemas (e.g., recognizing that "continuation" and "callback" share structure)

Each operation has a different protocol. See [[Building-Robust-Mental-Models]].

## The Schema Density Hypothesis

> *The velocity of new learning is proportional to the density of relevant schemas in LTM.*

This is the operational form of [[Long-Term-Working-Memory]]. It predicts:

- Early learning is slow (no schemas to absorb new material)
- Mid-stage learning accelerates (schemas compound)
- Expert learning is very fast (most new material is variation on known schemas)

This is why the first 6 months of a new subdomain feels like pushing through mud, and why the next 2 years feels like flying. The schemas are doing the work.

## The Two Failure Modes

### Failure mode 1: Schema-less accumulation
Reading 50 papers without writing synthesis notes. Produces:
- Vague familiarity with terminology
- Inability to recall specifics
- No transfer to new problems
- False sense of expertise

### Failure mode 2: Schema rigidity
Building one schema and applying it everywhere. Produces:
- "When all you have is a hammer" syndrome
- Misapplying patterns (e.g., trying to use Paxos for everything consensus-shaped)
- Inability to see when a different schema applies

The cure for both is the same: **deliberate schema integration** — explicitly connecting new schemas to existing ones, and explicitly testing when each applies.
