# Review and Refactor Cycle

> *Periodically re-evaluate your approach, not just your progress.*

---

## The Principle

Daily and weekly reviews adjust *what* you're learning. Quarterly reviews adjust *how* you're learning.

Without meta-review, you'll drift into:
- Inefficient methods (because they're familiar)
- Outdated assumptions (about the field, about yourself)
- Stale schemas (the field moved; your schemas didn't)
- Burnout signals you missed

The refactor cycle is the [[The-Learning-Loop|learning loop]] applied to itself.

---

## The Quarterly Review (every 3 months)

Block 4-6 hours. Take it seriously.

### Step 1 — Rest first (1 week before)

You cannot review your approach from inside it. Take a "consolidation week" — 50% load, no new material. Just sleep, exercise, review existing notes. Your brain needs to consolidate before it can meta-evaluate.

### Step 2 — Inventory (60 min)

Open your vault and inventory:

- How many concept notes? How many revised vs. draft?
- How many SR cards? Active vs. abandoned?
- How many build projects completed?
- How many papers read at Pass 2 vs. Pass 3?
- What's your SR retention rate?

Numbers, not feelings.

### Step 3 — Re-read the foundations (60 min)

Re-read [[MOC-Foundations]] notes. Your understanding will be different than 3 months ago.

Specifically re-read:
- [[Cognitive-Load-Theory]]
- [[Deliberate-Practice]]
- [[Selective-Ignorance]]

What did you internalize? What's still abstract? What are you violating?

### Step 4 — Evaluate each active topic (30 min per topic)

For each CS subdomain you've been learning:

- What schemas have I acquired?
- What schemas am I missing?
- What's my evidence of competence (builds completed, problems solved)?
- Where am I stuck?
- Should I continue, accelerate, slow down, or pivot?

### Step 5 — Audit your workflows (60 min)

For each workflow ([[The-Learning-Loop]], [[Weekly-Learning-Rhythm]], [[Knowledge-Consolidation-Workflow]], [[Build-to-Learn]]):

- Am I actually running it? (Check logs.)
- Is it producing results?
- What's the friction?
- Should I modify it?

Common findings:
- "I've been skipping retrieval practice — that's why retention is low"
- "I've been reading too much, building too little"
- "My triage is too permissive — Tier 1 list keeps growing"
- "I haven't run the weekly review in 6 weeks"

### Step 6 — Update your roadmap (30 min)

Open [[The-3-7-Year-Arc]]. Re-evaluate:

- Am I on track?
- What should I accelerate?
- What should I drop?
- What new direction should I add?

Update the [[Phase-1-Foundations|phase notes]] as needed.

### Step 7 — Commit to one major change (15 min)

Don't try to change everything. Pick *one* significant change for the next quarter:

- "I will implement every algorithm I learn, no exceptions"
- "I will reduce my Tier 1 list by 50%"
- "I will take a real rest day, every week, no exceptions"
- "I will add 30 min of retrieval practice to every block"

Write the change at the top of your [[Daily-Learning-Log]] template. Track it daily.

### Step 8 — Take a week off (1 week)

After the review, take a full week off (see [[Burnout-Prevention]]). Travel, hobbies, complete disengagement from CS.

Return refreshed. Execute the change.

## The Annual Review (once per year)

Once per year, do a deeper review:

### Step 1 — Inventory the year
- Hours of deep work
- Papers read
- Builds completed
- Schemas acquired (estimate)
- Major projects shipped

### Step 2 — Compare to last year
- What did I think I'd know that I don't?
- What do I know that I didn't expect to?
- What surprised me?

### Step 3 — Revise the multi-year plan
- Update [[The-3-7-Year-Arc]]
- Adjust the phases if needed
- Drop or add subdomains

### Step 4 — Re-read the vault
- Re-read your oldest notes
- Notice how your understanding has changed
- Update or archive notes that no longer reflect your understanding

### Step 5 — Take 2 weeks off
Seriously. Two weeks. Complete disengagement. You'll come back with new ideas.

## Signals You Need a Refactor

Don't wait for the quarterly review if you notice these:

- **Declining enthusiasm** for the work (sign of burnout or staleness)
- **Repeated failure** to make progress on a topic (sign of wrong approach)
- **Increasing friction** in your workflows (sign of accumulated cruft)
- **Plateau in skill** despite effort (sign of misdirected practice)
- **Boredom** with your current topics (sign you need a new challenge)
- **Reading the same thing repeatedly** without retention (sign of failed triage or missing schemas)

When you notice 2+ of these, run a mini-refactor: 2 hours, focused on the specific issue.

---

## Anti-Patterns

- ❌ Skipping the quarterly review ("too busy") — drift compounds
- ❌ Treating the review as a status report — it's for adjustment
- ❌ Making too many changes at once — pick one
- ❌ Not taking the rest week — defeats the consolidation
- ❌ Not committing to a specific change — vague intentions don't change behavior
