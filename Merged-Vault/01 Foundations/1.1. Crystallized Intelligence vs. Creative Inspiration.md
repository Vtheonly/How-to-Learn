# 1.1. Crystallized Intelligence vs. Creative Inspiration

## 1. Theoretical Foundations

To achieve reliable, on-demand cognitive performance, you must first dismantle the romanticized myth of the "creative spark." Relying on spontaneous flashes of insight to solve complex, high-stakes problems is a structurally flawed approach. 

Psychologists Raymond Cattell and John Horn proposed the investment theory of cognitive abilities, dividing general intelligence ($g$) into two distinct components:

    g[General Intelligence g] --> Gf[Fluid Intelligence Gf]
    g --> Gc[Crystallized Intelligence Gc]
    
    Gf --> Gf_Desc[Raw reasoning capacity, working memory, novel problem-solving, highly vulnerable to stress and fatigue]
    Gc --> Gc_Desc[Acquired knowledge, mental models, systemic procedures, highly resilient under physiological stress]
