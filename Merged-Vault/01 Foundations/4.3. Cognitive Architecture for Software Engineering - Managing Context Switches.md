# 4.3. Cognitive Architecture for Software Engineering: Managing Context-Switches

## 1. The Cost of the Developer Context-Switch

For a software engineer, a context-switch is the mental equivalent of a hard disk thrashing. 

When you are deeply focused on a complex coding task, your brain builds a highly sophisticated, temporary mental model of the system’s state, variables, and execution paths. This model is held entirely in your volatile **Working Memory**.
