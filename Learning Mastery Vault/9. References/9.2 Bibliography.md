# 9.2 Bibliography

This bibliography lists the key papers, books, and sources cited throughout the vault. Citations are grouped by topic.

## Cognitive Psychology — Core Techniques

### Testing Effect and Active Recall

- **Roediger, H. L., & Karpicke, J. D. (2006).** Test-enhanced learning: Taking memory tests improves long-term retention. *Psychological Science*, 17(3), 249-255. — The landmark study demonstrating the testing effect.
- **Karpicke, J. D., & Blunt, J. R. (2011).** Retrieval practice produces more learning than elaborative studying with concept mapping. *Science*, 331(6018), 772-775. — Retrieval practice beats concept mapping.
- **Spitzer, H. F. (1939).** Studies in retention. *Journal of Educational Psychology*, 30(9), 641-656. — One of the earliest demonstrations of the testing effect.
- **Adesope, O. O., Trevisan, D. A., & Sundararajan, N. (2017).** Rethinking the use of tests: A meta-analysis of practice testing. *Review of Educational Research*, 87(3), 659-701. — Meta-analysis confirming the testing effect.

### Spaced Repetition

- **Ebbinghaus, H. (1885).** *Memory: A contribution to experimental psychology.* — The original forgetting curve and spacing effect.
- **Cepeda, N. J., Pashler, H., Vul, E., Wixted, J. T., & Rohrer, D. (2006).** Distributed practice in verbal recall tasks: A review and quantitative synthesis. *Psychological Bulletin*, 132(3), 354-380. — Meta-analysis of 251 studies on the spacing effect.
- **Dunlosky, J., Rawson, K. A., Marsh, E. J., Nathan, M. J., & Willingham, D. T. (2013).** Improving students' learning with effective learning techniques. *Psychological Science in the Public Interest*, 14(1), 4-58. — The comprehensive review of learning techniques, ranking practice testing and distributed practice as "high utility."

### Pretesting and Hypercorrection

- **Kornell, N., Hayes, B. J., & Bjork, R. A. (2009).** Unsuccessful retrieval attempts enhance subsequent learning. *Journal of Experimental Psychology: Learning, Memory, and Cognition*, 35(4), 989-998. — The pretesting effect.
- **Butterfield, B., & Metcalfe, J. (2001).** Errors committed with high confidence are hypercorrected. *Journal of Experimental Psychology: Learning, Memory, and Cognition*, 27(6), 1491-1494. — The hypercorrection effect.

### Elaborative Interrogation

- **Dunlosky, J., et al. (2013).** (See above.) — Ranks elaborative interrogation as "moderate utility."

## Neuroscience — Memory and Plasticity

### Memory Systems

- **Atkinson, R. C., & Shiffrin, R. M. (1968).** Human memory: A proposed system and its control processes. In *The Psychology of Learning and Motivation* (Vol. 2). — The multi-store model of memory.
- **Cowan, N. (2001).** The magical number 4 in short-term memory: A reconsideration of mental storage capacity. *Behavioral and Brain Sciences*, 24(1), 87-114. — Working memory capacity revised to ~4 chunks.
- **Squire, L. R. (1992).** Declarative and nondeclarative memory: Multiple brain systems supporting learning and memory. *Journal of Cognitive Neuroscience*, 4(3), 232-243.

### Sleep and Consolidation

- **Yoo, S. S., Hu, P. T., Gujar, N., Jolesz, F. A., & Walker, M. P. (2007).** A deficit in the ability to form new human memories without sleep. *Nature Neuroscience*, 10(3), 385-392. — Sleep deprivation reduces hippocampal encoding capacity by ~40%.
- **Walker, M. P., & Stickgold, R. (2006).** Sleep, memory, and plasticity. *Annual Review of Psychology*, 57, 139-166. — Comprehensive review of sleep-dependent memory consolidation.
- **Diekelmann, S., & Born, J. (2010).** The memory function of sleep. *Nature Reviews Neuroscience*, 11(2), 114-126. — The mechanism of hippocampal replay and neocortical transfer.

### Neuroplasticity

- **Bliss, T. V. P., & Lømo, T. (1973).** Long-lasting potentiation of synaptic transmission in the dentate area of the anaesthetized rabbit following stimulation of the perforant path. *Journal of Physiology*, 232(2), 331-356. — The discovery of LTP.
- **Hebb, D. O. (1949).** *The Organization of Behavior.* — "Neurons that fire together, wire together."
- **Eriksson, P. S., Perfilieva, E., Björk-Eriksson, T., Alborn, A. M., Nordborg, C., Peterson, D. A., & Gage, F. H. (1998).** Neurogenesis in the adult human hippocampus. *Nature Medicine*, 4(11), 1313-1317. — Adult neurogenesis confirmed in humans.
- **Ratey, J. J., & Hagerman, E. (2008).** *Spark: The Revolutionary New Science of Exercise and the Brain.* — Exercise and BDNF.

### Attention and Focus

- **Mark, G., Gudith, D., & Klocko, A. (2008).** The cost of interrupted work: More speed and stress. *CHI Proceedings.* — Interruptions cost ~23 minutes of recovery.
- **Leroy, S. (2009).** Why is it so hard to do my work? The challenge of attention residue. *Organizational Science*, 20(3), 575-587. — Attention residue after task switching.
- **Ward, A. F., Duke, K., Gneezy, A., & Bos, M. W. (2017).** Brain drain: The mere presence of one's own smartphone reduces available cognitive capacity. *Journal of the Association for Consumer Research*, 2(2), 140-154. — The "phone on the desk" effect.

## Computer Science Education Research

### Code Comprehension and Tracing

- **Lister, R., Adams, E. S., Fitzgerald, S., Fone, W., Hamer, J., Lindholm, M., McCartney, R., Moström, J. E., & Sanders, K. (2004).** A multi-national study of reading and tracing skills in novice programmers. *ACM SIGCSE Bulletin*, 36(4), 119-150. — The landmark tracing study.

### Worked Examples and Cognitive Load

- **Sweller, J. (1988).** Cognitive load during problem solving: Effects on learning. *Cognitive Science*, 12(2), 257-285. — The original Cognitive Load Theory.
- **van Merriënboer, J. J. G., & Paas, F. (1990).** Automation and schema acquisition in learning elementary computer programming: Implications for the design of practice. *Computers in Human Behavior*, 6(3), 273-289. — The completion method.
- **Sweller, J., Ayres, P., & Kalyuga, S. (2011).** *Cognitive Load Theory.* Springer. — The comprehensive book on CLT.

### Parsons Problems

- **Parsons, D., & Haden, P. (2006).** Parson's programming puzzles: A fun and effective learning tool for first programming courses. *ACE Proceedings.* — The original Parsons Problems paper.
- **Leinonen, V., Hellas, A., et al. (2022).** Parsons Problems and beyond: Systematic literature review and empirical study designs. *ACM ITiCSE Proceedings.* — The systematic review.

### Notional Machines

- **Sorva, J. (2013).** Notional machines and introductory programming education. *ACM Transactions on Computing Education*, 13(2), 1-31. — The notional machine synthesis.

### Retrieval Practice in CS

- **Retrieval Practices Enhance Computational and Scientific Thinking Skills.** *Frontiers in Education* (2022). — Retrieval practice improves computational thinking.

## Educational Psychology — Heuristics

### SQ3R

- **Robinson, F. P. (1946).** *Effective Study.* Harper & Row. — The original SQ3R method.

### Pomodoro

- **Cirillo, F. (2006).** *The Pomodoro Technique.* — Official book on the technique.

### Mind Mapping

- **Farrand, P., Hussain, F., & Hennessy, E. (2002).** The efficacy of the mind map study technique. *Medical Education*, 36(5), 426-431. — Mind mapping efficacy study.
- **Buzan, T. (1974).** *Use Your Head.* — The original mind mapping book (note: makes many unsupported claims).

### Focus and Diffuse Modes

- **Oakley, B. (2014).** *A Mind for Numbers: How to Excel at Math and Science (Even If You Flunked Algebra).* TarcherPerigee. — Popularized focus/diffuse modes.
- **Andrews-Hanna, J. R. (2012).** The brain's default network and its adaptive role in internal mentation. *The Neuroscientist*, 18(3), 251-270. — Default Mode Network research.

### Diffuse Mode and Boredom

- **Bailey, C. (2018).** *Hyperfocus: How to Be More Focused in a Distracted World.* — Scatter focus and embracing boredom.
- **Twenge, J. M. (2017).** *iGen: Why Today's Super-Connected Kids Are Growing Up Less Rebellious, More Tolerant, Less Happy.* — Smartphone generation research.

## Debunking Pseudoscience

- **Pashler, H., McDaniel, M., Rohrer, D., & Bjork, R. (2008).** Learning styles: Concepts and evidence. *Psychological Science in the Public Interest*, 9(3), 105-119. — The learning styles neuromyth debunked.
- **Lilienfeld, S. O., Lynn, R., Ruscio, J., & Beyerstein, B. L. (2010).** *50 Great Myths of Popular Psychology.* — Comprehensive myth debunking.

## Books Worth Reading

### Learning Science (Popular)

- **Brown, P. C., Roediger, H. L., & McDaniel, M. A. (2014).** *Make It Stick: The Science of Successful Learning.* — The most accessible popular book on learning science.
- **Carey, B. (2014).** *How We Learn: The Surprising Truth About When, Where, and Why It Happens.* — Popular overview.
- **Oakley, B. (2014).** *A Mind for Numbers.* (See above.)

### Focus and Productivity

- **Newport, C. (2016).** *Deep Work: Rules for Focused Success in a Distracted World.* — Deep work principles.
- **Bailey, C. (2018).** *Hyperfocus.* (See above.)
- **Newport, C. (2019).** *Digital Minimalism: Choosing a Focused Life in a Noisy World.* — Reducing digital distraction.

### Sleep

- **Walker, M. P. (2017).** *Why We Sleep: Unlocking the Power of Sleep and Dreams.* — The most comprehensive popular book on sleep.

### Computer Science Education

- **Sorva, J. (2012).** *Programs as Data.* — Mental models in programming.
- **Pea, R. D. (1986).** Language-independent conceptual "bugs" in novice programming. *Journal of Educational Computing Research*, 2(1), 27-40. — Early misconceptions research.
